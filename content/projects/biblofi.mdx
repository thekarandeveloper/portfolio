## Overview

BiblioFi simplifies core library management workflows. Students can search for books, issue them, track returns, and move through the library workflow from one mobile interface.

The project was designed during an Infosys UI/UX internship in Mysore. I designed UX flows for the iOS Library Management System and collaborated with product owners and a team of 10 developers.

## Research

The core input was the SRS requirement set. The work was to translate those requirements into structured user flows and interface designs that a development team could build from.

That meant making book search, issue, and return workflows visible, ordered, and easy to hand off.

## Key Features

- UX structure and interface flows for an iOS-based library management system.
- Book search, issuing, and return workflows.
- Wireframes and structured user flows from SRS requirements.
- Collaboration with a team of 10 developers.

## Testing

The design work focused on clarity before polish: making each step understandable enough for product owners and developers to review, question, and implement.

## Learning

Leading developers is also a design challenge. Clear IA, handoff notes, and decision rationale kept the team aligned under a tight timeline.
